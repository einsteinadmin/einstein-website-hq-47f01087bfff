Mover Journey — Expert-Panel Audit

A 7-lens adversarial audit of the Mover Journey homepage concept — stress-testing KP's three concerns and finding the one real blocker before build commits.

2026-06-16 · 7-lens adversarial panel · Target: live hosted mockup ↗

Panel verdict — bottom line

Ship the concept — but not the pinned Camera Stage. Build the lighter peekaboo version, and fix one blocker first.

KP's loudest concern (SEO and content burial) is essentially a myth in this implementation. KP's quietest concern (build difficulty and maintenance) is understated and decisive. The one genuine blocker — flagged independently by 4 of 7 critics — is a one-line fix: the mid-floor "See my price" buttons scroll users down into more story instead of routing them to /quote/. We answer maximum buyer intent with maximum friction.

Fix that CTA, then build peekaboo-on-desktop — one engine, marketer-editable blocks, roughly 40% of the cost — and A/B it against the already-built static/reduced-motion version. The concept retains ~85–90% of its emotional payoff at a fraction of the cost and long-term risk.

KP's three concerns, adjudicated

The Scorecard

Each concern KP raised — run through all seven lenses and resolved against cited evidence.

Overblown Low severity

SEO / Content Pushed Down

Every floor is real server-rendered HTML — Googlebot reads it regardless of scroll position. Visually-deferred content is not SEO-deprioritized. The only below-fold pattern that actually hurts rankings is content injected by user action; this page animates content that's already in the DOM.

Real kernels to fix: Production plumbing gaps (meta description, canonical, JSON-LD, deep location footer links) — not the scroll journey itself.
Partly Real Medium severity

Distraction / Readability

A/B-testable and could cut either way. The dwell beats slow the eye where dense doubt-answering copy lives — that can reduce anxiety, not amplify it. One slice is non-debatable: Floor 2 is overloaded (two competing H2s, four accordions, three stat expanders, two-to-three quips — in a single viewport).

Action: Instrument dwell and CTA-click-by-floor; fix Floor 2 now. Don't wait on data for that one.
Real High severity

Technical / Mobile Build Difficulty

Confirmed and understated — the actual cost and risk driver. Two bespoke engines, hand-coded SVG hardcoded to floor count and order, vh-tuned fragility, a main-thread scroll loop. Jeff owns it forever; marketers can't edit it — which forfeits the core rationale for choosing WordPress FSE.

Counter-note: Scroll-linked (not scroll-jacked), and the JS-off / reduced-motion fallbacks are genuinely robust. Those specific fears are overblown. The cost/maintenance concern is not.
The one true blocker
Blocker — fix before any build decision

Repoint all mid-floor "See my price" CTAs (Floors 4, 3, 2) to /quote/

A user sold at Floor 4 who clicks the most prominent CTA is not taken to the quote form. The click fires an in-page jump that scrolls them down through the rest of the journey to an empty, aria-hidden, 1px div at the finale. Identical "See my price" labels on divergent destinations train then betray the user. For assistive-tech users, it routes focus into a nameless hidden target — a WCAG 2.4.3 / 4.1.2 failure.

This is a one-line href change. Highest ROI on the board. The #see-price scroll anchor can stay as an explicit B variant in a dedicated A/B test. It should never be the default.

Must-fix before build

Recommended build path

Peekaboo-on-Desktop + Rive + Static Control

Fix the cheap stuff first, then build the lighter version. Let data — not enthusiasm — decide whether the full cinematic build earns its keep.

The path

Fix the cheap stuff first (CTA + Floor 2 + hero speed claim), then build peekaboo-on-desktop as the production path. Drop the pinned Camera Stage; use the mobile "window between cards" pattern everywhere, each floor a real editable FSE block. Commission the characters in Rive — not hand-coded SVG or open-ended sprite sheets. Ship the already-built static/reduced-motion version as the A/B control and the guaranteed-fast, no-JS, SEO content layer. Instrument scroll-depth, CTA-click-by-floor, and time-to-first-CTA. Let the data — not internal enthusiasm — decide whether the motion earns its keep.

Why this path wins: Peekaboo-on-desktop retains ~85–90% of the "follow the couch down the building" hook and all the dwell-to-read pacing while deleting the single most expensive and fragile thing in the audit. Two engines collapse to one (the mobile engine already works). Content stays in normal flow, so focus order, reflow, and the three AA failures just work. Floors become marketer-editable blocks — restoring the WordPress FSE rationale.

Estimated cost: ~0.35–0.45× the Camera Stage build cost. Roughly 85–95% of measurable value retained, per every lens that assessed it. The only thing sacrificed is the cinematic "held-camera" wow moment — which is a preference to validate with user data, not a business requirement to build from.

Stack notes for KP / Jeff:

  • WP FSE block theme; each floor = reusable server-rendered block
  • Native scroll only — no pinning, no scroll-jack, no settle-assist for v1
  • Rive runtime for the 2-mover + couch scroll-scrubbed state machine (~6 poses)
  • Hero video: poster-LCP + lazy load ≤ 800KB
  • Ship FAQPage + LocalBusiness JSON-LD, meta description, canonical, OG tags, and deep per-market footer links
  • Keep the reduced-motion / JS-off static page as both the A/B control and the crawlable content layer

Open questions for KP / Jeff

Before Any Build Decision Locks

Seven questions the audit surfaced. Some are budget-level decisions; all are worth resolving before the build scope commits.

Is the homepage dev-owned or marketer-owned? If marketer-owned, it cannot be the pinned monolith — each floor must be a real editable FSE block. Resolve this first, or it surfaces later as a surprise change-request cost.
Fixed-bid quote: full Camera Stage vs. peekaboo-on-desktop. Does the real delta match the ~0.4× estimate? The build-cost difference should drive the decision more than aesthetic preference does.
Fixed-bid quote for the character pipeline as a Rive file (2 movers + couch, ~6 poses, scroll-scrubbed) before approving any animation build. This is the most cost-unbounded single line item in either path.
Will Jeff field-measure INP on a real low-end Android with the production animation layer, as a contract acceptance criterion? The current placeholder video makes the page fast; that won't hold at production fidelity without a specific spec.
Illustration style: polished-flat (Rive) or deliberately-rough ("South Park cutout")? A brand call for Emma to lock against the existing flat SVG aesthetic. A high-render character would clash with the building and truck art already on the page.
Is the pinned "held-camera" cinematic feel a business requirement, or a design preference? This decides whether to even evaluate keeping the Camera Stage. Internal enthusiasm is a useful signal but shouldn't be the deciding vote — validate the pattern with the target user (a high-intent, time-pressured quote-seeker) and A/B data.
Can KP own the production SEO plumbing as part of the build? This includes meta description, canonical, OG tags, FAQPage + LocalBusiness JSON-LD, and deep per-market footer links. These are largely independent of the homepage design — they're production plumbing that needs to ship regardless of which direction is chosen.
How the panel split

Seven Critics, One Synthesis

The panel was unusually well-aligned for an adversarial exercise — that convergence strengthens the overall verdict. Four points of tension, and how the chair resolved them.

CTA-bug severity

CRO, UX, and Copy all rated this a blocker. SEO and Brand didn't flag it (out of their scope). Chair sided with the majority: high risk, trivial fix, no reason to ship without it.

Distraction direction

CRO and UX argue that dwell beats can lift conversion by slowing the anxious eye. Brand and Copy worry that density taxes the user. Resolved as "partly real, A/B-testable," with Floor 2 pulled out as the one non-debatable fix — too many competing elements even under the most charitable reading.

Does the pin earn its keep?

Feasibility and Accessibility were the most critical (cost, fragility, three AA failures). Brand was most protective of the cinematic quality. Chair weighted feasibility most heavily, since it adjudicates KP's strongest real concern — and every lens independently rated peekaboo-on-desktop at 85–95% value retained. Almost nothing measurable is lost by dropping the pin.

Rive vs. hand-coded SVG

The Feasibility critic called hand-coded vanilla "the most expensive to maintain, not the cheapest" — for a production homepage, not a prototype. Chair sided with Rive for production. No critic argued to keep the hand-coded engine in production.

UX / Interaction NN/g heuristics 9 findings: 1 blocker · 2H · 4M · 2L

Brand Graphic Design 6 findings: 2H · 2M · 2L

Premium and on-brand where restrained (orange discipline, couch-as-protagonist, type hierarchy) — lock illustration to the existing flat style, escalate the couch across floors instead of repeating it, declutter Floor 2, and treat the legacy-WP seam as the number-one brand risk.

Accessibility WCAG 2.2 AA 9 findings: 3H · 4M · 1L · 1 nit

Strong bones (reduced-motion, JS-off, and semantic HTML are all genuinely robust), but the pinned stage adds three fixable AA failures — focus visibility, pause control, and reflow — all of which peekaboo-on-desktop erases at once.

Copywriting StoryBrand / messaging 6 findings: 1 blocker · 1H · 2M · 2L

Conversion-grade StoryBrand structure — answer-first H1, customer's fears as the hero's problem, Einstein as guide. The only blocker is the CTA that says "See my price" but scrolls to the bottom of the story instead of the form.
