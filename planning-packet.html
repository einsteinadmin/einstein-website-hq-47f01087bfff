2 · Live copy snapshot — the thesis mostly holds, with four asterisks

Pulled today from production. Verbatim copy for all 5 pages lives in research/live-site-copy.md — that's the raw material for the mockups. Here's the read.

Page-by-page

  • Homepage — 15 sections deep. The "6 Reasons" copy is the rewrite at its best; trust signals scattered across 4 different sections instead of one arc. Three differently-labeled CTAs competing.
  • Why Us (/why-us/) — sharp opener + the honest disqualifier ("Just moving a few boxes? We might not be your best fit"). But it sprawls to 9 differentiators vs. the homepage's 6 — two competing canon lists.
  • Location — North Austin (representative pick) — a content goldmine with zero architecture: 18 sections, 60+ full mover bios, 20+ FAQs on one scroll. The bios and local-living guide are exactly the "location pages with soul" material the research calls for — it's all there, just buried.
  • Straightforward Pricing — the strongest persuasion content on the site (line-item quote comparison, coverage matrix, "Moving Cost Nightmares") trapped in plain two-column tables with ✅/❌ glyphs.
  • Service — Apartment Movers (representative pick) — the weak one. Visibly older copy stratum ("mind-blowing customer service," "No one is better") mixed with one new paragraph; structurally an SEO doorway template; "Book Your Move Now" appears 6+ times.

The four asterisks on "the copy is good"

  • Gap "Smart Move System" is never named on any of the 5 pages. Only fragments ("Smart moves are built on…", Smart Move Checklist). The brand story the redesign exists to deliver isn't actually on the live site yet.
  • Contradiction Verified live 6/11: homepage reason #2 says "simple, flat-rate pricing and no hidden fees," and the shared final-CTA band ("…flat-rate pricing, no hidden fees, and over 55+ awards won") runs on the homepage, pricing page, AND location page — while those same pricing/location pages explain "Straightforward Hourly Rates." The intent is one flat all-inclusive hourly rate, but "flat-rate pricing" reads as fixed-total-price. Wording fix, e.g. "one all-inclusive hourly rate" — and the mockups should use the corrected phrasing.
  • Drift Proof numbers vary unlabeled: 8,100+ reviews (sitewide) vs 1,400+ (N. Austin Google) vs "100,000+ moves" vs "1,000s+ of moves." All true at different scopes — mockups should label scope.
  • Bugs Four production copy bugs captured verbatim (e.g. the pricing page's broken "optional" nightmare headline, "try Einstein Moving a try"). Worth a punch list to KP regardless of the redesign.

Also telling: emoji doing icon/badge/callout work all over the site (🏅⭐💡🤷📍) — the copy improvising visual components the theme doesn't have. That's the design-constraint thesis written in the copy itself.

De facto component library already in the copy: comparison table (3 variants) · ✅ included-in-rate checklist (2 near-duplicate sources) · 47-second quote block · trust subtext line ("No deposit · Free Cancellation · Instant Online Quotes") · final CTA band · review cards with move context · award narrative cards · service grid · location grid. Emma's component extraction has raw material before we draw anything.

4 · Four candidate shapes for the tool

The non-obvious call underneath all of these: the workshop-tool pattern we've proven (quote funnel, field app) is built for reviewing a known change set. This project is different — it's generating and choosing between design directions. So the shape question is real, not cosmetic. The 5-variations pattern (brettfromdj) is Shape A as promised — an option, not the default.

SHAPE A

Variations Gallery — 5 treatments per page, side by side

For each of the 5 page types, one HTML file renders ~5 full design variations of the same page with the same real copy. Reviewers flip between them and pick a winner (or parts of winners) per page.

For

  • Maximum divergence — exactly what "get ALL the ideas out" asks for
  • Direct comparison is the easiest review mode for non-designers
  • Fable 5 can genuinely generate distinct treatments fast

Against

  • 5 pages × 5 variations = 25 full page builds — variations risk going shallow (same layout, different paint)
  • Doesn't natively serve Emma's component extraction — winners are whole pages, components stay implicit
  • Decision mechanics get messy ("I like 2's hero and 4's pricing table") with no structured way to capture that
SHAPE B

Classic workshop tool — one build per page + proposed-change cards

The proven idiom: rebuild each of the 5 pages once in a single design direction; every departure from the current site is a change card (Keep/Deny, trade-off, notes, export). KP reviews like they reviewed the quote funnel.

For

  • Pattern is proven with KP — they just green-lit the quote-funnel workshop in this exact idiom
  • Export = a real build spec; lowest tooling risk
  • Change cards can carry component tags for Emma

Against

  • Presumes the design direction is already chosen — it isn't. Five concepts exist; nobody has picked
  • One direction baked in = the exploration Laura asked for never happens
  • Keep/Deny on "the whole new hero" is a clumsy unit of decision for design work
SHAPE C

Two-stage: direction shoot-out, then page build-out

Stage 1: render 3 finalists from the existing 5 mockup-direction concepts as full-fidelity HOMEPAGES with real copy — a direction shoot-out. Cameron + KP pick/blend one. Stage 2: apply the winning direction across all 5 page types in the workshop idiom, with component tagging for Emma.

For

  • Matches how design actually converges: direction first, then application
  • Reuses the existing concept work instead of re-deriving it — the wireframes become real
  • Bounded: 3 homepage builds, then 5 pages once — ~8 builds total vs. Shape A's 25
  • Stage 1 alone is a strong artifact for next week even if travel interrupts Stage 2

Against

  • Two decision gates = more elapsed time, and KP is about to go dark (Laura out, closed week of July 4)
  • Homepage-only shoot-out may under-test how a direction handles a pricing table or a 60-bio roster
SHAPE D

Component-first — variant library + assembled pages (mix and match)

Since Emma's sprint-2 job is extracting components, lead with them: build the key blocks (hero, trust band, process story, pricing table, review wall, location intro…) in 2–3 treatments each, then assemble the 5 pages from them. The tool lets reviewers swap component variants in place per page slot and tags every region as reusable-component vs. page-specific.

For

  • Most directly serves the stated sprint-2 goal — the export IS a component inventory with chosen treatments
  • Variation at the component level = combinatorial richness for far less build than Shape A
  • Thinks exactly like the FSE block theme the site will be built on

Against

  • Risks losing the gestalt — great pages are designed wholes, and mix-and-match invites frankenpages
  • Heaviest engineering of the four (slot-swapping machinery)
  • Component treatments still need a direction to be designed IN — smuggles the Shape C question back in
My lean (for the interview, not a decision): Shape C with a Shape D export — direction shoot-out on the homepage first, then build out the 5 pages in the winning direction with every region tagged component-vs-page-specific so Emma's extraction is reading a labeled map, not archaeology. Shape A stays available as the Stage 1 mechanic if you'd rather shoot out 5 than 3.

Default: design-first, copy-corrected — keep the live copy as the base, fix the contradictions/bugs silently, and flag any real copy *proposals* as their own tagged change type so KP sees them as suggestions, not faits accomplis. Separately: send KP the bug punch list now regardless.

You alone → then Emma? A live working session with Laura/Emma? Async with each KP person exporting their own decisions? 6 · Photography and video: what do we actually have?
Every strong reference leans on real footage (crews, trucks, drone). The Brand Kit bans stock. For mockups: do we have a real asset library to pull from (Austin L's footage? drone shots? archive photos of you and Paul?), or do the mocks use honest placeholder frames ("60-SEC ORIGIN FILM HERE") like the wireframes did?
Default: real assets where they exist, labeled placeholder frames where they don't — never stock, never AI-generated people.

Angles I don't think anyone's raised

7 · How much motion belongs in the mocks?
The theme constraint is partly about *feel* — scroll-pinned chapters, parallax, the scroll-based mover animation you floated to KP on 6/3. Static mocks can't sell motion; motion-heavy mocks take longer and can distract from layout decisions. There's also a middle path: build one page with full motion as the "feel proof," keep the rest static.
Default: one motion showcase (the homepage), rest static-with-notes.
8 · Mobile: first-class or footnote?
Majority of mover traffic is mobile, the Brand Kit says mobile-first — but mocking every variation responsively roughly doubles the work, and KP's component extraction may only need desktop + a mobile note per component. A toggle-width preview in the tool is a cheap middle ground.
Default: responsive builds (they're HTML anyway) with a device-width toggle in the tool — not separate mobile designs.
9 · What does Emma actually need from the export?
Sprint 2 is hers. Nobody has specified what "extract components" consumes: a labeled component inventory? Annotated HTML she can inspect? Token-mapped CSS (the kit's tokens.json → theme.json pipeline)? Worth asking at the 2:30 today — it determines whether component tagging is a nice-to-have or the spine of the tool.
Default until KP answers: every region tagged component-vs-page-specific, with a component inventory export (name, where used, which treatment won).

Four days. Stage 1 of Shape C (3 homepage treatments) is achievable by then; all five pages in any shape is not — unless we keep building during travel and KP reviews async. Should the plan explicitly target "direction locked before you leave, build-out continues remotely"?
Default: yes — direction shoot-out reviewable by Sun 6/14, build-out async after.
