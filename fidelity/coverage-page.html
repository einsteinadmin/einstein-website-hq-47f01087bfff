What “60¢ a pound” really means.

Here’s how the industry actually works: by law, a mover only owes you 60 cents per pound if they break something. It’s called released-value coverage, it’s the legal minimum, and it has nothing to do with what your things are worth. A 150-pound sofa? That’s a $90 check — no matter what the sofa cost.

Most movers will sell you an upgrade — often around $200. But read the fine print: most paid upgrades are still per-pound. Your $1,500 laptop weighs five pounds. Even on a paid tier, that’s about $15. You paid extra to still be underpaid.

This isn’t an attack — it’s just the math nobody shows you before move day. So we will:

Covering your things by the pound is like insuring your wedding ring by how much it weighs — not what it’s worth.
