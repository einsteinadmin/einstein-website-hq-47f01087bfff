Bottom line

The build plan in plain English

The six supporting pages are not six separate builds — they're one component library wrapped around shared chrome (nav, footer, sticky CTA bar, trust strip), so the real work is building roughly a dozen reusable blocks once and copy-swapping them per page. Build the conversion spine first, in this order: the comparison receipt (your strongest honesty argument — with the two locked fixes: delete the 'Quote = Invoice' line and keep the read on the honesty axis, not base-rate), the honest-copy hero chassis, the fear-to-pillar Smart Move System bands, the inclusions ledger, the crew cards, and the FAQ-with-JSON-LD. Two non-negotiables thread every page: the H1 must carry the literal keyword (movers + Texas/Tampa) because keyword-thin brand-voice headlines are our biggest SEO miss, and every number must be scope-labeled (city vs the company-wide 8,100+) to stay honest and avoid number-drift. The location and service pages are the SEO workhorses but they're blocked on two net-new builds the catalog doesn't have yet — a dedicated 'services offered' grid / per-service page template, and a single per-branch data file to drive all 10 cities — plus the structured-data emitters (LocalBusiness, Service, Organization, AggregateRating) that turn content into rankings. The cheapest highest-leverage unblock is the production SVG icon set: every page currently stubs icons with placeholder emoji and can't ship without it. Final cleanup items before launch: a real mobile hamburger (all five mockups are missing it), wiring city links to the new branch pages instead of the old site, and recoloring the orange damage-coverage panel to pass contrast.
